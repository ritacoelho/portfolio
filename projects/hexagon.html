Hexagon: Enterprise Scale Design

Strategic UX and product design across Hexagon's geospatial and industrial technology suite: design systems, complex workflow redesign and cross-team research at global scale.

Company

Hexagon AB

Period

2022 to Present

My role

UX Designer

Location

Zurich, Switzerland

NDA notice: Specific product names, screens and metrics have been anonymised. The narrative reflects real work; the details have been generalised in line with my NDA obligations.

Context

Hexagon is a global leader in digital reality solutions: sensors, software and autonomous technology. The products I work on are used by millions of operators across surveying, construction, manufacturing and public safety sectors worldwide. Complexity is the baseline.

The work

My work at Hexagon spans three interconnected streams: design systems (establishing and scaling shared component libraries across product teams); workflow redesign (rethinking complex, multi-step operator tasks to reduce cognitive load and error rate); and research (planning and facilitating user research that feeds directly into roadmap decisions).

A consistent theme across all three: designing for expert users. Hexagon's customers are domain specialists with years of experience in their field. Good enterprise UX for these users looks very different from consumer product design; it needs to support complex mental models, not simplify them away.

My approach

I work embedded in a cross-functional team alongside product managers, engineers and domain experts. Research at Hexagon means talking to surveyors on construction sites, operators running safety systems and field technicians who rely on our software to do dangerous jobs accurately. That proximity to the work shapes everything I design.

Impact

Specific metrics are covered under NDA. Broadly: design system adoption reduced inconsistency across product surfaces; workflow redesign work contributed directly to measurable reductions in task completion time for key operator journeys; research programmes established consistent user insight processes where none previously existed.
