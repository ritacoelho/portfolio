Pilot: Bridging the Gap Between University and Work

A live industry brief from Atlassian: design an intelligent, AI-powered project management tool to help students and new employees manage work across university, the workplace, and personal life, all in one place.

Client

Atlassian

Year

2023

My role

Lead UX Designer

Team

4 designers

The problem

Atlassian wanted to expand into a younger demographic (students and new employees) at a time when it was investing heavily in AI through Atlassian Intelligence. The challenge: existing tools like Asana, Notion, and Monday.com weren't built for people who need to juggle academic deadlines, early-career responsibilities, and personal commitments all at once. Students were duct-taping together Google Drive, Canvas, Discord, and WhatsApp to get through the week.

Brief: Design an intelligent project management tool for students and new employees that enhances productivity and team collaboration, sitting within the Atlassian ecosystem.

User research surfaced a clear signal: 95% of participants wanted a single tool to handle diverse tasks, and 86% said task and time management was their biggest pain point. Group communication and engagement with project tools were close behind. Three "how might we" questions shaped our direction: how might we adapt the experience to personal needs, help users allocate time more accurately, and keep them coming back?

Competitive analysis covered direct competitors (Asana, Notion, Monday.com), non-direct competitors with relevant patterns (GitHub, Figma, Canva), and the tools students were already using day-to-day. None of the existing solutions bridged the student-to-employee context: they either served professionals or academics, not both. This gap became the foundation for three design opportunities: personalised productivity enhancement, engagement through seamless integration, and contextual collaboration.

My role

I was lead UX designer across the project, working in a team of four. I owned the research synthesis, led the ideation sessions (Crazy 8s, group brainstorming, decision matrix), and designed core interaction flows including the AI project creation model and the notification system. I contributed to all three design iterations and presented the final prototype and rationale to Atlassian's product and design team.

Solution

Pilot is an intelligent project management tool that lets users manage work across academic, workplace, and personal contexts from a single interface. The homepage surfaces AI-recommended tasks ordered by priority across all active projects. The project page includes an AI assistant that generates a full task structure from an assignment brief; users can review, edit, or accept the suggestions before committing. The calendar uses AI to schedule group meetings around team availability and detects clashes with concrete resolution options.

Key design decisions: a task recommendation engine that surfaces what matters now across all projects, not just one; an AI project scaffold that reduces the friction of starting from scratch; agenda clash resolution that gives users real alternatives rather than just flagging a conflict; and a notification system that tracks postponed tasks and lets users request help from teammates directly. Premium features unlock per-user analytics and extended calendar filtering.

Both satisfaction metrics improved consistently across all three testing rounds. The prototype was presented to Atlassian's product and design team at the end of the brief. The AI project scaffold (which generates a task structure directly from a brief) was identified as the standout differentiator, addressing a gap none of Atlassian's existing products currently fill.
