MAP Mima Sonic Link: Music as Public Space

A group project to design a mobile experience letting individuals shape the soundscape and light choreography of a public catenary installation in real time. I contributed interaction design and the MQTT communication layer, required by the brief.

Master's project

The problem

Public spaces are usually passive. You move through them; they don't respond to you. MAP Mima, a cultural venue built around music, wanted to explore what it would mean to make a physical space genuinely reactive to the people inside it. The brief: design an interactive system that connects mobile phones to a large-scale catenary LED installation and responds to personal music identity.

Design challenge: How do you give an individual a meaningful sense of agency over a shared, public environment, without it feeling chaotic or exclusionary to others in the space?

Install

The group's research covered service design for shared spaces, reactive public art precedents (teamLab, Random International) and the psychology of collective experience. The key insight: individual agency feels most meaningful when its effects are visible but not dominant, contribution without control.

My contribution

This was a group project. My contributions were interaction design (the mobile app's UX and UI, from early sketches through to high-fidelity screens) and the MQTT communication layer that was a specific technical requirement of the brief. MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport) is a lightweight publish-subscribe protocol designed for constrained environments; implementing it was part of what we were asked to demonstrate.

I chose MQTT specifically for its low latency and ability to handle multiple simultaneous client connections, critical for a public installation where several users might be interacting at once. It was the right tool for the constraint.

Solution

The Sonic Link app presents users with a set of sound palettes, each mapped to a specific colour temperature and light pattern. When a user selects a palette, the choice is published via MQTT to the installation's broker, which translates it into LED commands across the catenary lights above the venue floor.

The result: the space breathes differently depending on who's in it and what they're listening to. Individual choices contribute to a collective atmosphere, personal without being solipsistic.
